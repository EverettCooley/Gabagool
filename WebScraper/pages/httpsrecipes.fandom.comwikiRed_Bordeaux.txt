Pronounced bore-DOUGH. A regional red wine produced in the Bordeaux region of France. Bordeaux is also a major city in France. There are only six grape varietals allowed in the production of Red Bordeaux. Those six are: 
 
 Cabernet Franc 
 Cabernet Sauvignon 
 Carmener  (virtually extinct in the Bordeaux region) 
 Malbec 
 Merlot , and 
 Petit Verdot . 
 The Bordeaux is the largest region of wine growing in the world. There are 6 major appellations of the Bordeaux region: 
 
 Barsac 
 Graves 
 Medoc 
 Pomerol 
 St. Emilion , and 
 Sauternes . 
 Wines that are labeled “Bordeaux” are inferior to those labeled with an appellation.
