Description : 
 Here's a finger food that's great for a house full of kids or to use as an appetizer. Blue corn tortilla chips add more flavor to this casual favorite. This dish works best when it is served immediately. For 4 people, just add soup and you have a complete meal.
 
 Contributed by  Healthy Recipes for Diabetic Friends Y-Group 
 Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 10 minutes 
 Original recipe 
 Makes 8 servings. 
 Ingredients : 
 1 pound lean  ground beef  or lean ground round 
 1½ teaspoons  cumin 
 1 teaspoon  chili powder 
 1 (15-ounce) can low-salt  black beans , rinsed and drained 
 1 (10-ounce) bag blue corn  tortilla chips , divided 
 1 cup reduced-fat Mexican  cheese  blend, divided 
 1 cup shredded  lettuce , divided 
 1 cup chopped  tomatoes , divided 
 chopped  green onions  (optional) 
 chopped fresh  cilantro  (optional) 
 sliced  black olives  (optional) 
 salsa  (optional) 
 low-fat  sour cream  (optional) 
 Directions : 
 Cook beef, cumin and chili powder in a large skillet over medium- high heat 5 to 10 minutes or until browned, stirring to crumble. 
 Drain well. 
 Stir in beans and cook until thoroughly heated. 
 Layer ½ tortilla chips, ½ cup beef mixture, ½ cup cheese, ½ cup lettuce and ½ cup tomato on a large platter. 
 Repeat with remaining ingredients. 
 Garnish with green onions, cilantro, olives, salsa and sour cream, if desired. 
 Nutritional information : 
 Per serving:
 
 Calories 320 (33% from fat) | fat 12.0g (sat 3.3g) | protein 20.4g | carb 34.9g | fiber 6.1g | chol 31mg | iron 3.7g | sodium 284mg
