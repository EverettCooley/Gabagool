       Creole Beignets     
 A  beignet  is a deep-fried pastry that has been rolled in sugar. The name "beignet" comes from the French word for "fritter". Beignets are popular in New Orleans, where they are also referred to as donuts.
 
 Ingredients : 
 1 cup of  water 
 1 cup of  milk 
 1  egg , beaten 
 3 cups of  flour 
 2 tablespoons of  baking powder 
 1 teaspoon of  salt 
 2 teaspoons of  sugar 
 oil  for frying 
 confectioner's sugar 
 Directions : 
 Mix  water ,  milk ,  egg ; add  flour ,  baking powder ,  salt  and 2 teaspoons of  sugar  and mix until smooth. 
 Drop by spoonfuls into 2 inches of hot  oil  at 375 degrees; drain on paper towels and sprinkle with confectioners  sugar .
