Description 
 This popular African dish is fun to say and fun to eat. Serve fufu in place of mashed potatoes for a sweet surprise.
 
 Fruits & Veggies Matter  by the U.S. Centers for Disease Control—original source of recipe, U.S. government resource in the public domain 
 Cook Time : 30 minutes 
 Serves : 4 
 Ingredients 
 5 cups  water 
 4 ripe  plantains   [1] 
 1 Tbsp  butter 
 1 clove of  garlic , minced 
 4  green onions  (scallions), chopped 
 Directions 
 Peel plantains and slice into 1-inch pieces. 
 Bring water to a boil. Add plantain pieces and cook on medium-high for 20 minutes until most water is absorbed. 
 Add butter and garlic to plantains. Mash with a potato masher. 
 Garnish with green onions and serve. 
 Variation 
 For a creamy texture, add ¼ cup of  skim milk  or  soy milk  to plantains when mashing. 
 Notes 
 
 ↑   Plantains look like large bananas and can be found in the produce section of many grocery stores. Ripe plantains will have black skins.
