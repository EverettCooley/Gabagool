 Description : 
 This recipe came from an estate sale. I obtained it when I purchased the family collection from the Wadley Estate in Allen, Texas in 1990.
 
 Contributed by  Catsrecipes Y-Group 
 Ingredients : 
 1 can  lager  beer 
 1 cup spicy  barbecue sauce 
 2 tablespoons  maple syrup 
 2 tablespoons  soy sauce 
 2 racks  pork back ribs 
 Directions : 
 In a large, wide saucepan or pasta pot, stir and mix beer with barbecue sauce, maple syrup, and soy sauce. 
 Slice pork into 4 rib portions with the butcher knife or the cleaver, then immerse in beer mixture. Cover and bring to a boil over high heat then reduce heat to medium low.Â  
 Simmer, partially covered, and turning ribs occasionally for 1 hour. 
 Remove ribs to a large bowl. Bring liquid in a saucepan to a boil then reduce heat to low. 
 Simmer uncovered stirring often until thickened about 20 minutes. 
 When ready to barbecue, oil the grill and heat to moderate. 
 Heat sauce in a microwave until warm, then brush ribs with sauce and grill uncovered for 2 minutes. 
 Turn and brush with sauce again. Continue grilling turning often and basting with sauce until richly glazed about 10 minutes. 
 Place ribs in a large bowl and drizzle with any remaining sauce. Stir to coat. Place on a platter. 
 Use any sauce left in bowl for dipping. Serve hot and plain over rice.
