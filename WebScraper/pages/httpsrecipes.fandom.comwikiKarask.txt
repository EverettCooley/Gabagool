Description : 
 One of Estonia's dessert items is this cake-like  barley  bread. The recipe is hundreds of years old and was forgotten for a long time. It is now making its way back. The recipe is easy and the taste is simple but different, soft and delicious. This is what Estonia’s wealthier ancestors ate for special occasions.
 Serve karask hot with this cranberry cream sauce. This serving will be enough for 10 – 12 people.
 
 Ingredients : 
 500 g plain yoghurt or sour cream 
 1  egg 
 ½ teaspoon  salt 
 3 tablespoon  sugar 
 60 g  butter , melted 
 100 g  whole wheat flour 
 200 g  barley flour 
 ½ teaspoon  baking soda 
 150 g  carrot  (grated finely) 
 20 g  butter , for sautéing 
 Cranberry cream sauce : 
 200 g plain  yoghurt 
 40 g  sugar 
 80 g  cranberries 
 150 g  whipping cream 
 Directions : 
 Grate the  carrot , sauté with  butter  on a pan, and let it cool. 
 Mix soda and flours. 
 rozbijeme eggs , add  sour cream , sugar,  salt . 
 Now in this mixture, add the  flour  mixture. 
 In the end, add melted  butter  and  carrots  and mix very well. 
 Pour the whole mixture in a baking dish and bake at 180°C for 35 – 40 minutes. 
 
 
 
 Cranberry cream sauce : 
 Mix  sugar  and yoghurt, until sugar is dissolved. 
 Whip the  cream  until soft and mix in the yoghurt. 
 Mash the  cranberries  and add to the mixture.
